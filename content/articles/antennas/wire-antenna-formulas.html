Wire Antenna Formulas & Calculator

Quick reference for calculating wire antenna lengths for amateur radio bands. All lengths in feet unless otherwise noted.

Basic Formulas

Half-Wave Dipole

Total length (both legs):

Length (feet) = 468 / Frequency (MHz)

Each leg is half this length.

Quarter-Wave Vertical

Length (feet) = 234 / Frequency (MHz)

Full-Wave Loop

Length (feet) = 1005 / Frequency (MHz)

End-Fed Half-Wave (EFHW)

Length (feet) = 468 / Frequency (MHz)

Same as dipole but fed at one end with a 49:1 transformer.

Random Wire Lengths to Avoid

Avoid these lengths as they present difficult impedances:

  • Half-wavelength multiples on any band you want to use
  • Specific lengths: 42', 63', 84', 126', 168', 252'

Recommended lengths for multi-band random wire: 29', 35.5', 41', 58', 71', 84', 135'

Adjustment Tips

  • Start long: Cut 2-3% longer than calculated, trim to tune
  • Height affects resonance: Lower = slightly longer wire needed
  • Insulation: Wire with insulation needs ~3% shorter length
  • Tune with SWR meter: Target SWR < 2:1 across desired portion of band
  • Fold excess: If too long, fold back and tape rather than cut

Quick Field Calculation

For dipole in feet:  468 ÷ MHz
For dipole in meters: 143 ÷ MHz
For vertical in feet: 234 ÷ MHz
For vertical in meters: 71.5 ÷ MHz
