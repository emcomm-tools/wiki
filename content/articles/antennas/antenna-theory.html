Resonance

ğŸ¯ Resonance

When an antenna's inductive and capacitive reactances cancel out, leaving only resistance. The antenna is most efficient at its resonant frequency.

Tuning for Resonance

  • Antenna too long: Resonant frequency too LOW, trim the ends
  • Antenna too short: Resonant frequency too HIGH, add length
  • SWR dip: Lowest SWR occurs at resonance (if impedance matches)
Always cut long! When building antennas, start a few percent longer than calculated. It's easy to trim wire shorter; adding length is harder!

When to Use a Tuner

  • Multi-band antenna (dipole, doublet, end-fed)
  • Using antenna outside its resonant frequency
  • Random wire antennas
  • Ladder line fed antennas

Practical Considerations

Height Matters!

  • Higher = better for most antennas
  • At Â½Î» height, dipole has maximum low-angle radiation (DX)
  • Lower heights favor higher angles (NVIS)
  • Verticals need good ground/radial systems

Ground Effects

  • Real ground is not perfect - affects patterns and impedance
  • Radials improve vertical antenna efficiency
  • Horizontal antennas less affected by poor ground
  • Salt water is best "ground" for verticals

Antenna Modeling
