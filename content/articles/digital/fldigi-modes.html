Digital Modes Overview

Fldigi (Fast Light Digital) supports dozens of digital modes. Each mode has different characteristics for speed, bandwidth, and weak-signal performance. Understanding these helps you choose the right mode for conditions.

Key Characteristics

CharacteristicWhat It Means
Bandwidth How wide the signal is (Hz). Narrower = less interference, wider = faster/more robust
Speed Characters per second or WPM. Faster modes need stronger signals
Error Correction (FEC) Forward Error Correction - mode can fix errors without retransmission
Interleaving Spreads data over time - helps with fading but adds latency
Sensitivity How weak a signal can be decoded (dB below noise)

PSK Modes

PSK31 - The Classic

Phase Shift Keying at 31.25 baud. The most popular HF digital mode for casual keyboard-to-keyboard chat.

Speed~50 WPM
Bandwidth31 Hz
FECNone (BPSK) / Yes (QPSK)
Sensitivity-10 dB SNR

Characteristics

  • Very narrow bandwidth - many signals fit in small spectrum
  • Good weak signal performance
  • Typing speed matches transmission - real-time chat
  • Susceptible to QSB (fading) and QRM
  • Varicode encoding - common letters sent faster

Variants

  • BPSK31 - Standard, no error correction
  • QPSK31 - With FEC, better for marginal conditions

PSK63 / PSK125 / PSK250

Faster PSK variants for better conditions.

PSK63~100 WPM, 63 Hz
PSK125~200 WPM, 125 Hz
PSK250~400 WPM, 250 Hz
  • Use when signals are strong
  • Good for nets with lots of traffic
  • Less popular - fewer stations to contact

PSK31 Tip: Keep your ALC meter showing NO deflection. PSK is very sensitive to over-driving - even a little ALC action creates splatter that interferes with nearby signals.

RTTY (Radioteletype)

RTTY - The Original

Frequency Shift Keying, dating back to the 1930s. Still popular for contests!

Speed45.45 baud (~60 WPM)
Shift170 Hz (standard)
Bandwidth~250 Hz
FECNone

Characteristics

  • Two tones: Mark and Space (FSK)
  • 5-bit Baudot code - UPPERCASE only, limited characters
  • Very popular for contesting (RTTY Roundup, etc.)
  • Tolerant of slight mistuning
  • No error correction - characters can be garbled

Settings

  • Shift: 170 Hz (amateur standard)
  • Baud: 45.45 (standard) or 75
  • Polarity: Normal or Reverse (try both if garbled)

MFSK Modes

MFSK (Multi-Frequency Shift Keying)

Uses multiple tones for better performance than simple FSK.

MFSK16

Speed~42 WPM
Bandwidth316 Hz
FECYes
Sensitivity-13 dB SNR
  • 16 tones, good weak signal mode
  • Built-in FEC for error correction
  • Good for HF weak signal work

MFSK32 / MFSK64 / MFSK128

  • MFSK32: ~63 WPM, 500 Hz - faster, needs better signal
  • MFSK64: ~120 WPM, 1000 Hz - much faster
  • MFSK128: ~240 WPM, 2000 Hz - very fast, good conditions only

Olivia

Olivia - The Weak Signal Champion

MFSK mode optimized for extremely weak and difficult conditions. Excellent for EmComm!

Popular8/250, 16/500, 32/1000
FECYes (strong)
InterleavingYes
Sensitivity-14 to -18 dB SNR

Naming Convention: Tones/Bandwidth

  • Olivia 8/250: 8 tones, 250 Hz bandwidth - ~12 WPM
  • Olivia 8/500: 8 tones, 500 Hz - ~24 WPM
  • Olivia 16/500: 16 tones, 500 Hz - ~31 WPM
  • Olivia 16/1000: 16 tones, 1000 Hz - ~47 WPM
  • Olivia 32/1000: 32 tones, 1000 Hz - ~63 WPM

Characteristics

  • Outstanding weak signal performance
  • Very tolerant of QRM and QSB
  • Long interleaving adds ~3 second latency
  • Both stations must use same Olivia variant!
  • Popular for EmComm and DX
EmComm Recommendation: Olivia 8/500 or 16/500 are excellent choices for emergency communications - robust enough for poor conditions, fast enough to be practical.

Contestia

Contestia - Fast Olivia

Similar to Olivia but with shorter interleaving - less latency, slightly less robust.

Popular8/250, 16/500, 32/1000
FECYes
Latency~1 second (vs 3 for Olivia)
  • Good compromise: faster than Olivia, more robust than PSK
  • Same tone/bandwidth options as Olivia
  • Better for conversational use (less delay)

Thor

Thor - IFK+ Mode

Incremental Frequency Keying Plus - self-synchronizing, doesn't need preamble.

VariantsThor 4, 8, 11, 16, 22, 25, 50, 100
FECYes
SyncSelf-synchronizing

Popular Variants

  • Thor 11: ~11 WPM, excellent weak signal
  • Thor 16: ~22 WPM, good balance
  • Thor 22: ~31 WPM, faster
  • Thor 50: ~68 WPM, faster yet

Characteristics

  • No preamble needed - decodes immediately
  • Good for short transmissions
  • Tolerant of Doppler (good for meteor scatter)
  • FEC with soft-decision decoding

DominoEX

DominoEX - IFK Mode

Incremental Frequency Keying - similar to Thor but different implementation.

VariantsDomEX 4, 5, 8, 11, 16, 22, 44, 88
FECOptional (DomEX FEC variants)
  • DominoEX 11: Good weak signal, ~20 WPM
  • DominoEX 22: Faster, ~40 WPM
  • Fast synchronization
  • Nibble-based encoding

MT63

MT63 - Wideband Robust

64-tone mode designed for HF keyboard chat in poor conditions.

Bandwidth500, 1000, or 2000 Hz
Speed~100 WPM (1000 Hz)
FECYes (strong)
InterleavingLong or Short

Characteristics

  • Very robust against interference
  • Wide bandwidth allows data to survive selective fading
  • Long interleaving version more robust but ~10 sec latency
  • Short interleaving ~2 sec latency, less robust
  • Not compatible with contest situations (too wide)
MT63 Width: At 1000 Hz or 2000 Hz, MT63 is VERY wide. Use only when clear spectrum is available. Not appropriate for crowded band conditions.
