Why Space Weather Matters

The sun drives HF propagation. Solar activity ionizes Earth's atmosphere, creating the layers that reflect radio waves. Understanding solar indices helps you:

  • Predict band conditions - Know which bands will be open
  • Plan operations - Schedule contacts when conditions favor your path
  • Troubleshoot - Understand why bands are dead or noisy
  • EmComm planning - Know if HF will be reliable

Solar Flux Index (SFI)

‚òÄÔ∏è Solar Flux Index (10.7 cm Flux)

Measures solar radio emissions at 2800 MHz (10.7 cm wavelength). Higher values indicate more solar activity and better ionization for HF propagation.

SFI ValueConditionsEffect on HF
< 70Very LowLow bands only (40m-80m), high bands dead
70 - 90LowLow/mid bands, high bands marginal
90 - 120ModerateMost bands usable, 15m opening
120 - 150GoodExcellent propagation, 10m-12m opening
> 150ExcellentAll bands open, worldwide DX on 10m

Key Points

  • Measured daily in Penticton, BC, Canada
  • Range: ~65 (solar minimum) to 300+ (solar maximum)
  • Higher SFI = Higher MUF (Maximum Usable Frequency)
  • Takes weeks/months to change significantly

üß≠ K-Index (Planetary Kp)

Measures geomagnetic disturbance over 3-hour periods. Lower is better for HF!

K-IndexConditionHF Effect
0InactiveBest conditions
1Very QuietExcellent
2QuietVery good
3UnsettledGood, some degradation
4ActiveFair, noticeable degradation
5Minor Storm (G1)Degraded, especially polar paths
6Moderate Storm (G2)Poor, auroral absorption
7Strong Storm (G3)Very poor
8Severe Storm (G4)Major disruption
9Extreme Storm (G5)HF blackout possible

Key Points

  • Updated every 3 hours (8 readings per day)
  • Quasi-logarithmic scale (each step is ~2x)
  • K ‚â• 4 generally means degraded propagation
  • High-latitude paths affected first
  • Aurora can occur at K ‚â• 5 (good for VHF!)
K-Index Timing: Effects lag behind solar events. A CME hitting Earth causes K-index to spike 1-3 days after the flare. Watch for rising K after major solar events.

  • Number after letter indicates intensity within class
  • Example: M5.2 is stronger than M1.0

  • Effects are immediate (speed of light)
  • Only affects dayside of Earth

Radio Blackouts (R-Scale)

NOAA's Radio Blackout scale rates solar flare effects on HF:

ScaleFlareDescriptionHF EffectDuration
R1 M1 Minor Weak or minor degradation of HF on sunlit side ~1 hour
R2 M5 Moderate Limited blackout of HF on sunlit side Tens of minutes
R3 X1 Strong Wide area HF blackout on sunlit side ~1 hour
R4 X10 Severe HF blackout on most of sunlit side 1-2 hours
R5 X20 Extreme Complete HF blackout on sunlit side Several hours
Immediate vs. Delayed: R-scale (flare) effects are immediate (8 minutes after flare). G-scale (storm) effects are delayed 1-3 days (CME travel time to Earth).
