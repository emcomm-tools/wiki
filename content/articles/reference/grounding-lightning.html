Grounding & Lightning Safety

Proper grounding protects you, your equipment, and your home from lightning and RF hazards. This guide covers station grounding, lightning protection, and safety best practices.

⚡ LIGHTNING KILLS ⚡

No grounding system provides 100% protection from a direct lightning strike. The best protection is disconnecting antennas and power during electrical storms.

Why Grounding Matters

Grounding Serves Multiple Purposes

PurposeWhat It Does
Safety (Electrical) Provides path for fault currents, prevents shock
Lightning Protection Diverts lightning energy to earth (not through your house)
RF Ground Provides return path for antenna systems, reduces RFI
Static Discharge Bleeds off static buildup before it damages equipment
Noise Reduction Reduces received noise and interference

Types of Grounds

Different Grounds for Different Purposes

TypePurposeRequirements
AC Safety Ground Electrical code safety Per NEC, green wire in outlet
Lightning Ground Divert lightning energy Low impedance, short path to earth
RF Ground Antenna system reference Low RF impedance (may be radials)
Equipment Ground Common reference for gear Bond all equipment together
Critical: All grounds must ultimately connect together (bonding). Separate, unconnected grounds can create dangerous potential differences during a lightning strike.

Lightning Arrestor Installation

✅ Arrestor Checklist

  • ☐ Mount at or before building entry
  • ☐ Use bulkhead/panel mount type
  • ☐ Ground connection as SHORT as possible
  • ☐ Use #6 AWG minimum to ground rod
  • ☐ NO sharp bends in ground wire
  • ☐ Ground rod within 2 feet of arrestor
  • ☐ Bond to electrical service ground
Sharp Bends: Lightning follows the path of least inductance, not resistance. Sharp bends in ground wires increase inductance and can cause lightning to jump to other paths (like your equipment!).

Multiple Ground Rods

For better lightning protection, use multiple ground rods:

  • Space rods at least 8 feet apart (equal to rod length)
  • Bond all rods together with #6 AWG or larger
  • Create a ring around the tower base if possible
  • Bury connecting wire 6-12 inches deep

Bonding & Single Point Ground

⚠️ NEC Requirements

Per National Electrical Code (NEC) Article 810:

  • Antenna discharge units (arrestors) required
  • All grounds must bond to electrical service ground
  • Minimum #10 AWG copper for antenna ground
  • Ground electrode conductor per NEC 250

Key Principle: Everything connects to ONE point, and that point connects to earth. This prevents ground loops and ensures all equipment rises to the same potential during a surge.

Portable/Field Operations

Field Station Grounding

Portable stations have different grounding considerations:

SituationGrounding Approach
POTA/SOTA Counterpoise wires, vehicle chassis, ground stake
EmComm Field Ground rod + counterpoise, generator grounding
Vehicle Mobile Vehicle chassis is the ground plane
Temporary Base Ground rod(s), bond to building if possible

Field Ground Kit

  • 4-foot copper ground rod (portable)
  • Ground rod driver or hammer
  • #10 AWG stranded wire (25+ feet)
  • Alligator clips or clamps
  • Counterpoise wires (quarter-wave lengths)
  • Ground strap for equipment bonding
Field Lightning Safety: During thunderstorms, STOP operating, disconnect antennas, and seek shelter. A portable mast is a lightning rod!

Thunderstorm Safety Procedure

  1. Monitor weather - stop operating when storms approach
  2. Disconnect antenna feedlines from equipment
  3. Move away from antenna/mast
  4. Seek shelter in vehicle or building
  5. Wait 30 minutes after last lightning before resuming

Installation Guide

Ground Rod Installation

  1. Choose location near antenna entry point
  2. Check for underground utilities before digging!
  3. Drive rod until only 4-6" remains above ground
  4. If rock prevents full depth, use two rods at angle or horizontal
  5. Attach ground clamp securely
  6. Apply anti-oxidant compound to connections
  7. Run ground wire with no sharp bends
  8. Connect to single point ground
  9. Bond to electrical service ground

Improving Ground Conductivity

If soil is dry or rocky:

  • Use longer ground rods (10 ft)
  • Use multiple rods, bonded together
  • Add ground radials buried 6-12"
  • Treat soil with ground enhancement compound
  • Water the ground rod area (temporary help)

Testing Your Ground

Ground Resistance Testing

ApplicationTarget Resistance
Commercial/Industrial < 5 ohms
Residential (NEC) < 25 ohms
Amateur Station (goal) < 10 ohms
Lightning Protection < 10 ohms

Testing Methods

  • Fall-of-Potential: Professional 3-point test (most accurate)
  • Clamp-on Meter: Measures ground loop resistance
  • Simple Ohmmeter: Between ground rod and distant point (rough estimate)

Visual Inspection Checklist

✅ Annual Ground Inspection

  • ☐ Check all connections for corrosion
  • ☐ Verify clamps are tight
  • ☐ Look for damaged or frayed wire
  • ☐ Ensure ground rods haven't loosened
  • ☐ Check arrestors for damage/burn marks
  • ☐ Verify bonding connections intact
  • ☐ Test continuity with meter
