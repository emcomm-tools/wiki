NATO Phonetic Alphabet

The NATO phonetic alphabet is used worldwide by amateur radio operators to spell out callsigns and messages clearly, especially under poor propagation conditions.

Example: Spelling a Callsign

VA2OPS would be spoken as:

"Victor Alfa Two Oscar Papa Sierra"

Tips for Clear Communication

  • Speak slowly and clearly
  • Pause slightly between each word
  • Use phonetics when repeating important information
  • On HF, phonetics are almost always necessary for callsigns
  • On VHF/UHF with good signal, phonetics may not be needed
