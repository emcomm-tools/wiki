Message Handling

BREAKSeparates parts of a message (preamble/address/text)
CORRECTIONI made an error, correction follows
WRONGYour last transmission was incorrect
VERIFYCheck and confirm with originator
READ BACKRepeat this message back to me
I READ BACKHere is my readback of your message
RELAYTransmit this message to (callsign)
RELAY TOPass this to (station)
TIMETime follows in hours and minutes
AUTHENTICATEStation must verify identity

Ending Communication

OVEREnd of my transmission, expecting reply
OUTEnd of communication, no reply expected
CLEARI am done, frequency is available
NOTHING HEARDNo response received from called station
RADIO CHECKWhat is my signal strength and readability?
LOUD AND CLEARYour signal is strong and readable
Never say "OVER AND OUT" - These are contradictory. OVER means you expect a reply, OUT means you don't. Use one or the other!

Signal Reports (RST)

RST = Readability, Strength, Tone. Use RS for voice (no Tone), RST for CW.

R - Readability (1-5)

1Unreadable
2Barely readable, occasional words distinguishable
3Readable with considerable difficulty
4Readable with practically no difficulty
5Perfectly readable

S - Strength (1-9)

1Faint, barely perceptible
2Very weak
3Weak
4Fair
5Fairly good
6Good
7Moderately strong
8Strong
9Extremely strong

T - Tone (CW only, 1-9)

1Sixty-cycle AC, very rough
5Ripple modulated, musical
9Perfect tone, no trace of ripple
Examples:
"You're 5-9" = Perfectly readable, extremely strong (voice)
"You're 5-7-9" = Perfectly readable, moderately strong, perfect tone (CW)
"You're 3-3" = Readable with difficulty, weak signal
Be Honest: Don't give everyone "5-9" automatically. Accurate reports help operators know their true signal quality.

Usage Examples

Spelling a Callsign

VA2OPS:
"Victor Alfa Two Oscar Papa Sierra"

Spelling a Name

SYLVAIN:
"I spell: Sierra Yankee Lima Victor Alfa India November"

Giving a Frequency

7.185 MHz:
"Seven point one eight five megahertz" or
"Seven decimal one eight five"

Correcting an Error

"The frequency is 14.250... CORRECTION... 14.225 megahertz"

Requesting a Fill

"SAY AGAIN WORD AFTER 'SHELTER'"
(Asking sender to repeat the word that came after "shelter")

Handling Poor Conditions

"CONDITIONS POOR, WORDS TWICE"
(Now both stations send each phrase twice)

Emergency Break-In

"BREAK BREAK - This is W1ABC with emergency traffic!"
(Used to interrupt a net for urgent traffic)
