Why Keep a Log?

  • Legal requirement (some countries) - Canada and some others require logs
  • Award tracking - DXCC, WAS, VUCC need confirmed contacts
  • Personal record - Remember your QSOs over the years
  • Performance analysis - What bands/modes work best for you?
  • QSL management - Track sent/received cards
  • Contest scoring - Contests require accurate logs
  • Duplicate checking - Know if you've worked someone before
Canada (ISED): Canadian amateurs ARE required to keep a log of all transmissions. The log must be retained for at least one year.

UTC Time: Always log in UTC (Coordinated Universal Time, same as GMT/Zulu). This avoids confusion with time zones and daylight saving. Your radio probably has a UTC clock setting.

ADIF Format

ADIF (Amateur Data Interchange Format) is the standard format for exchanging log data between programs. Almost all logging software can import/export ADIF.

Sample ADIF Record

<CALL:5>W1ABC <QSO_DATE:8>20260109 <TIME_ON:4>1435 <BAND:3>20M <FREQ:8>14.25000 <MODE:3>SSB <RST_SENT:2>59 <RST_RCVD:2>57 <NAME:4>John <QTH:6>Boston <GRIDSQUARE:4>FN42 <EOR>

QSL means "I confirm receipt" in Q-code. QSL cards are postcards confirming amateur radio contacts - a tradition dating back to the 1920s!

Logbook of The World (LoTW)

LoTW is ARRL's electronic QSL system. It's the gold standard for award credit (DXCC, WAS, VUCC). Free to use!

Getting Started with LoTW

  1. Request certificate - arrl.org/lotw, verify identity
  2. Install TQSL - Software to sign and upload logs
  3. Configure callsign certificate - Links to your license
  4. Sign your log - TQSL digitally signs ADIF file
  5. Upload to LoTW - Through TQSL or logging software
  6. Check for matches - When both stations upload, it's confirmed!

LoTW Key Points

  • Both stations must upload for confirmation
  • Accepted for ARRL awards (DXCC, WAS, VUCC)
  • Cryptographically signed - very trustworthy
  • No physical cards needed
  • Widely used - many DX stations upload

Best Practices

  • Log immediately - Don't rely on memory
  • Use UTC - Avoids time zone confusion
  • Backup your log - ADIF export regularly
  • Upload to LoTW - Even if you also send cards
  • Keep paper backup - For field ops or computer failures
  • Accurate data - Wrong time/date means no confirmation
Contest Logging: During contests, use dedicated contest software (N1MM, N3FJP) - it's optimized for speed and includes multiplier tracking, dupe checking, and Cabrillo export.
