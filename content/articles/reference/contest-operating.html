What is Contesting?

Contesting (also called "radiosport") is competitive amateur radio - making as many contacts as possible within a defined time period. It's like a radio marathon!

Score = QSO Points √ó Multipliers

More contacts + More multipliers = Higher score!

Basic Concept

  • Time-limited - Usually 24-48 hours
  • Exchange information - Signal report + something else
  • Log everything - Accurate logging is critical
  • Multipliers - States, countries, zones boost your score
  • Categories - Single-op, multi-op, QRP, assisted, etc.

The Exchange

Each contest has a specific exchange - the information you must send and receive for a valid contact.

Example: CQ World Wide DX Contest

Exchange: RST + CQ Zone

CQ CONTEST VA2OPS VA2OPS W1ABC W1ABC 59 05 ‚Üê You send RST 59, Zone 05 59 05 ‚Üê He sends RST 59, Zone 05 TU VA2OPS ‚Üê Thanks, done, next!

Example: ARRL Field Day

Exchange: Category + ARRL Section

CQ FIELD DAY VA2OPS VA2OPS W1ABC W1ABC 2A CT ‚Üê You send 2A (2 transmitters, club), CT section 3A WMA ‚Üê He sends 3A, Western MA TU QRZ VA2OPS ‚Üê Thanks, QRZ = next caller

Example: State QSO Party

Exchange: RST + State/Province or County (if in-state)

CQ PAQP VA2OPS ‚Üê CQ Pennsylvania QSO Party VA2OPS K3ABC K3ABC 59 QC ‚Üê You send RST + Quebec 59 CHESTER ‚Üê In-state station sends county TU

Operating Techniques

CQ-ing (Running)

Call CQ on a clear frequency and let stations come to you:

CQ CONTEST CQ CONTEST VA2OPS VA2OPS CONTEST [Listen] W1ABC W1ABC 59 05 59 05 TU VA2OPS QRZ ‚Üê "QRZ" invites next caller

Search & Pounce (S&P)

Tune across the band and answer stations calling CQ:

  • Good for beginners - less pressure
  • Hear the exchange before you call
  • Can cherry-pick multipliers
  • Slower rate than running

üéØ S&P Tips

  • Listen to one or two complete QSOs first
  • Know the exchange before you call
  • Give your call once, clearly
  • Be ready with your exchange
  • If not heard, wait and try again

Basic Strategy

üìà Maximize Your Score

  1. Multipliers are gold - New states/countries multiply your whole score
  2. Rate matters - More QSOs = more points
  3. Run when you can - Higher rate than S&P
  4. S&P for multipliers - Hunt for new mults between runs
  5. Work the openings - Be on when propagation peaks
  6. Don't waste time - Move on from hopeless pileups

Getting Started

Your First Contest

  1. Pick a beginner-friendly contest - State QSO Party, Field Day
  2. Read the rules! - Know the exchange before starting
  3. Install logging software - N1MM Logger+ is free
  4. Configure your entry category - Single-op, your power level
  5. Start with Search & Pounce - Less pressure
  6. Don't worry about score - Just have fun and learn
  7. Submit your log - Even if only 10 QSOs!
Beginner Tip: Start with a State QSO Party. They're shorter (usually 12-24 hours), have simpler exchanges, and operators are friendly to newcomers. Everyone wants to work you for multiplier credit!
