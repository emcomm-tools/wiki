Choosing Coax

üéØ Quick Selection Guide

ApplicationRecommendedWhy
HF Base Station RG-213 or LMR-400 Low loss, handles power
HF Portable/POTA RG-8X Good balance of loss/weight
VHF/UHF Base LMR-400 Low loss critical at VHF+
VHF/UHF Mobile RG-8X or LMR-240 Flexible, reasonable loss
Short Jumpers (< 6ft) RG-58 OK Loss minimal in short runs
Repeater Site LMR-600 or Hardline Lowest loss for long vertical runs
Rule of Thumb: For VHF/UHF, spend money on good coax (LMR-400). The loss savings are worth more than an amplifier that just compensates for coax loss!

Adapter Basics

Common adapters to keep in your kit:

  • PL-259 to BNC (both male and female)
  • BNC to SMA
  • N to PL-259
  • SMA to BNC
  • Barrel connectors (same type both ends)
Adapter Loss: Each adapter adds a small amount of loss and another potential failure point. Minimize adapters when possible, especially at VHF/UHF.

Installation Tips

Connector Installation

  1. Measure twice, cut once
  2. Use proper stripping dimensions for your connector
  3. Don't nick the center conductor
  4. Ensure shield doesn't touch center conductor
  5. Solder properly - hot and quick, don't melt dielectric
  6. Inspect before use

Weatherproofing

  • Coax Seal: Moldable putty, easy to remove
  • Self-fusing tape: Stretchy, fuses to itself
  • Heat shrink: With adhesive lining
  • Drip loop: Let water run off before entering
  • Point connectors down when possible

Routing Best Practices

  • Avoid sharp bends (minimum bend radius = 10√ó diameter)
  • Don't crush or kink coax
  • Support coax on long vertical runs
  • Keep away from power lines
  • Use grommets through walls
  • Ground shield at entry point
