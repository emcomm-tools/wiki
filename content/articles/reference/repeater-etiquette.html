Repeater Basics

How Repeaters Work

  • Offset: You transmit on one frequency, repeater outputs on another
  • CTCSS/PL Tone: Many repeaters require a tone to access
  • Timeout Timer: Limits transmission length (usually 3 minutes)
  • Courtesy Tone: Beep indicating repeater is ready for next transmission
  • Tail: Brief period after courtesy tone before repeater drops

Making Calls

Calling a Specific Station

// Calling someone specific "W1ABC, this is VA2OPS" // If no answer after a few seconds: "W1ABC, VA2OPS calling" // After 2-3 attempts with no response, try later

Calling CQ (General Call)

// Keep it short on repeater - not like HF! "This is VA2OPS, listening" // Or: "VA2OPS monitoring" // NOT this (too long for repeater): "CQ CQ CQ, this is VA2OPS, Victor Alfa Two Oscar Papa Sierra, calling CQ and standing by"

Announcing Your Presence

// Quick kerchunk check (listen first!) "VA2OPS" // Better - with intention: "VA2OPS mobile monitoring" // Even better: "VA2OPS mobile, Montreal to Quebec City, monitoring"
Don't "Kerchunk": Keying the repeater without identifying (just to see if it works) is illegal and annoying. Always identify with your callsign.

Joining Conversations

Proper Way to Break In

// Wait for pause after courtesy tone, then: "VA2OPS" // Or: "Break, VA2OPS" // If they acknowledge: "Thanks for the break. I wanted to add that..."

If Your Break Is Acknowledged

  • Thank them for the break
  • State your purpose briefly
  • If just listening, say "No traffic, just wanted to say hello"
  • Don't take over the conversation - they were there first

If They Don't Acknowledge

  • They may not have heard you - try again on next pause
  • After 2-3 attempts, they may be in a private conversation - wait
  • Don't keep interrupting repeatedly
For Emergencies: Say "Break Break" or "Break Emergency" - this signals urgent traffic and should always be acknowledged immediately.

Courtesy Practices

The Pause

After each transmission, pause briefly (1-2 seconds) before the next person transmits:

  • Allows others to break in if needed
  • Lets repeater drop carrier momentarily (helps identify)
  • Gives time-out timer a break on long QSOs
  • Courtesy to those who want to use the repeater

Transmission Length

  • Keep it reasonable - 1-2 minutes typical
  • Watch the timer - Don't time out!
  • Long topics? - Break them into shorter transmissions
  • Remember others - Repeater is shared resource

Do's and Don'ts

‚úÖ DO

  • Listen before transmitting
  • Wait for courtesy tone
  • Keep transmissions brief
  • Identify properly
  • Be welcoming to new hams
  • Use phonetics when helpful
  • Pause between transmissions
  • Yield to emergency traffic
  • Be polite and courteous
  • Monitor before calling

‚ùå DON'T

  • Kerchunk without ID
  • Transmit over others (doubling)
  • Use CB lingo ("10-4," "what's your 20")
  • Discuss politics or religion controversially
  • Use profanity
  • Play music
  • Conduct business communications
  • Monopolize the repeater
  • Argue or be confrontational
  • Transmit while moving through dead spots

Special Situations

üîä Handling "Doubling"

When two stations transmit simultaneously:

  • Both stations should stop transmitting
  • Wait a moment, then one station goes ahead
  • Say "Go ahead [callsign]" to yield
  • Don't just keep trying to talk over each other

üì± When You're Mobile

  • Announce you're mobile: "VA2OPS mobile"
  • Let others know if you might lose signal
  • If signal is breaking up, others will let you know
  • Pull over for long or important conversations
  • Don't transmit through tunnels or dead spots

üîá Handling Interference

  • Don't acknowledge intentional interference
  • Continue your conversation normally
  • Move to another repeater if needed
  • Report persistent interference to repeater trustee
  • Never engage with "trolls" or jammers

üëã New Ham Calling

When a new/nervous ham calls:

  • Be welcoming! We were all new once
  • Answer them even if just briefly
  • Gently correct mistakes - don't embarrass them
  • Encourage them to come back
  • Remember: First experiences shape future activity

Emergency Procedures

Emergencies Always Have Priority! All other traffic should cease immediately when emergency traffic is announced.

If You Have an Emergency

// Break into ongoing QSO: "BREAK BREAK - Emergency!" "BREAK - Emergency traffic!" // When acknowledged: "This is VA2OPS. I'm at Highway 10 mile marker 45, there's a multi-vehicle accident. Requesting someone call 911."

If You Hear an Emergency

  1. Stop transmitting immediately
  2. Listen for details (location, nature, needs)
  3. Assist if you can - call 911, relay information
  4. Stay off frequency unless you can help
  5. Follow instructions from emergency station or net control

After the Emergency

  • Emergency station will announce "clear" when done
  • Normal operations can resume
  • Thank those who helped
