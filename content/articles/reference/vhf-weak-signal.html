What is Weak Signal?

Weak signal VHF/UHF operation uses SSB, CW, and digital modes (instead of FM) to work much greater distances than repeaters allow. It's a completely different world from FM!

Why Weak Signal?

  • DX! - Work stations 500-2000+ km away
  • Propagation excitement - Tropo, sporadic E, meteor scatter
  • Less crowded - Vast empty spectrum to explore
  • Technical challenge - Antenna gain, low noise, precision
  • EME (Moonbounce) - Bounce signals off the Moon!
  • Grid square chasing - VUCC award

Modes of Operation

üìª SSB (Single Sideband)

Primary voice mode for weak signal. USB on all VHF/UHF bands.

  • Bandwidth: ~2.4 kHz (much narrower than FM)
  • Advantage: All power in one sideband
  • Typical QSO: Callsigns + grid squares + signal report
  • Polarization: Horizontal for terrestrial

üì° CW (Morse Code)

Even narrower bandwidth, works in weaker conditions than SSB.

  • Bandwidth: ~100-500 Hz
  • Advantage: Works 3-6 dB below SSB threshold
  • Used for: EME, extreme weak signal

üíª Digital Modes

FT8, MSK144, JT65, Q65 - computer-decoded modes for extreme conditions.

  • FT8: -20 dB SNR, 15-second cycles, popular
  • MSK144: Meteor scatter optimized
  • JT65/Q65: EME and extreme tropo

VHF/UHF Propagation

üå°Ô∏è Tropospheric Ducting ("Tropo")

Temperature inversion traps signals in a duct, allowing long-distance propagation.

  • Bands: 2m, 70cm, higher bands
  • Distance: 500-2000+ km
  • When: High pressure, stable weather, coastal areas
  • Duration: Hours to days
  • Check: William Hepburn's tropo forecast maps

‚òÑÔ∏è Sporadic E (Es)

Ionized patches in E-layer reflect VHF signals. Unpredictable but exciting!

  • Bands: 6m (primary), sometimes 2m
  • Distance: 1000-2500 km typical
  • When: Late spring/early summer (May-August) peak
  • Duration: Minutes to hours
  • Signature: Sudden, strong signals, then gone

üå† Meteor Scatter (MS)

Meteor trails ionize briefly, reflecting VHF signals. Requires special modes.

  • Bands: 6m, 2m (best)
  • Distance: 1000-2200 km
  • When: Anytime, better during showers (Perseids, Geminids)
  • Duration: Bursts of 0.1-10 seconds
  • Mode: MSK144 designed for this!

üåô EME (Earth-Moon-Earth / Moonbounce)

Bounce signals off the Moon! Ultimate weak signal challenge.

  • Bands: 2m, 70cm, 23cm, higher
  • Distance: Worldwide (via the Moon!)
  • When: Moon above horizon at both ends
  • Path loss: ~250 dB (need big antennas!)
  • Mode: CW, JT65, Q65

üåà Aurora

Aurora borealis/australis reflects VHF signals. Distinctive "ghostly" sound.

  • Bands: 6m, 2m, sometimes 70cm
  • Distance: 1000-2000 km
  • When: Geomagnetic storms (K-index 5+)
  • Sound: Raspy, "auroral flutter"
  • Mode: CW easiest, SSB readable but rough

Equipment

üìª All-Mode Radio

  • IC-9700 (2m/70cm/23cm)
  • IC-705 (portable all-band)
  • FT-991A (HF+VHF+UHF)
  • TS-2000 (older but capable)

üì° Antennas

  • Yagi (most common)
  • Long yagi for EME
  • Loop yagi
  • Horizontal polarization!

üîä Preamp (LNA)

  • Tower-mounted preferred
  • Low noise figure (0.3-0.5 dB)
  • Helps weak signals
  • SSB Electronics, DX Engineering

üîß Extras

  • Rotator (point antenna)
  • Coax: Low loss (LMR-400+)
  • Power: 50-100W typical
  • Sequencer (for preamp)

Polarization Matters! Weak signal VHF uses horizontal polarization. A vertical antenna can't work horizontal stations effectively (20+ dB loss!). Mount your yagi with elements horizontal.

Operating Practices

Calling CQ

SSB WEAK SIGNAL CQ "CQ CQ CQ, this is Victor Alpha Two Oscar Papa Sierra, grid Foxtrot November Three Five, CQ" Or shorter: "CQ VA2OPS FN35" Listen for reply, then move off calling frequency for QSO

Standard Exchange

InformationExampleNotes
CallsignVA2OPSUse phonetics if needed
Grid SquareFN354 or 6 character
Signal Report55, 57, 59Honest reports help!

Operating Tips

  • Move off calling frequency! - After contact, QSY to another frequency
  • Give honest reports - "59" isn't always honest; use full scale
  • Grid squares are essential - Everyone exchanges grids
  • Listen for beacons - Indicates band open
  • Use low power first - Don't splatter with 100W on quiet band
  • Watch propagation forecasts - Plan operations around openings

  • 144 MHz: 100 grids for basic
  • 432 MHz: 100 grids
  • Higher bands: 50 grids (harder!)
