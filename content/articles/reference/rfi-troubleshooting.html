Systematic Approach

  1. Characterize the noise
    • What does it sound like? (buzz, hash, clicks, raspy)
    • What frequencies affected?
    • Continuous or intermittent?
    • Time pattern? (day/night, specific times)
  2. Is it in your house?
    • Kill main breaker - noise gone? It's yours.
    • Turn off breakers one by one
    • Unplug devices on problem circuit
  3. Locate with portable receiver
    • AM radio, tune to quiet spot
    • Walk around, signal strength increases near source
    • Use small loop for direction finding
  4. Confirm the source
    • Unplug suspected device
    • Noise stops? Found it!
    • Document for solution planning
The Main Breaker Test: Turn off your main breaker. If noise disappears, the source is in your house. If noise remains, it's external (neighbor, power line, etc.).

Grounding for RFI

Good grounding helps prevent both causing and receiving RFI:

  • Single Point Ground - All equipment grounds to one point
  • Short ground leads - Long wires are antennas at RF!
  • Bond everything - Eliminate ground loops
  • Coax shield grounded - At antenna AND at entry point
See also: Grounding & Lightning Safety for complete grounding guidance.

Dealing with Neighbors

If Your Signal Causes Interference

  1. Be proactive - Introduce yourself before problems arise
  2. Stay calm and friendly - You need their cooperation
  3. Offer to help - Fix the problem at their end
  4. Provide filters - High-pass filter for TV usually solves it
  5. Document everything - Times, frequencies, what you tried
  6. Know the rules - Most consumer devices aren't protected from interference

If Neighbor's Device Causes RFI

  1. Identify the source - Be certain before approaching
  2. Approach politely - "I'm having trouble with my radio..."
  3. Offer solutions - Ferrite chokes are cheap and easy
  4. If uncooperative - Contact utility (power line) or FCC/ISED
Power Line Noise: If RFI is from utility equipment (arcing, bad insulator), report it to your power company. They're required to fix it. Document with recordings if possible.
