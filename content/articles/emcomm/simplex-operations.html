What is Simplex?

Simplex means transmitting and receiving on the same frequency. Both stations talk directly to each other without any infrastructure in between.

  • No repeater needed - Works when repeaters are down
  • Direct path - Radio to radio
  • Same frequency - TX and RX on identical frequency
  • Limited range - Typically line-of-sight

Why Simplex Matters for EmComm

üö® When Repeaters Fail

During disasters, repeaters may be:

  • Destroyed - Tower/equipment damage
  • Without power - Backup batteries depleted
  • Overloaded - Too many users
  • Out of range - You're beyond coverage
  • Restricted - Access controlled during emergency

Simplex always works if you can hear each other!

Simplex Advantages

  • ‚úÖ No infrastructure required
  • ‚úÖ Works in remote areas
  • ‚úÖ Private (shorter range = fewer listeners)
  • ‚úÖ Lower power often sufficient
  • ‚úÖ No repeater timeout to worry about
  • ‚úÖ Available when repeaters are down

Simplex Limitations

  • ‚ùå Limited range (typically 5-30 miles)
  • ‚ùå Terrain dependent
  • ‚ùå Requires coordination (both on same freq)
  • ‚ùå No "store and forward"

Factors Affecting Range

  • Antenna height - Higher is better (significantly!)
  • Antenna type - Gain antenna vs rubber duck
  • Power - More helps, but antenna height matters more
  • Terrain - Hills, buildings block signals
  • Frequency - 2m travels slightly farther than 70cm
  • Band conditions - Tropospheric enhancement can help
Height Wins: Raising your antenna 10 feet often does more than doubling your power. Get that antenna up!

Operating Procedures

üìû Making Contact on Calling Frequency

  1. Listen first - Is the frequency clear?
  2. Call CQ or specific station:
    "CQ CQ, this is VA2OPS, Victor Alfa Two Oscar Papa Sierra, listening on 146.52"
  3. Wait for response
  4. When contact made, QSY:
    "W1ABC, let's move to 146.55, one four six point five five"
  5. Move to working frequency and continue conversation

üìç Monitoring/Calling for Local Activity

"Is there any mobile or base station in the Granby area? This is VA2OPS monitoring 146.52"

üÜò Emergency on Simplex

"BREAK BREAK - Emergency traffic - This is VA2OPS at Highway 10 mile marker 45, reporting multi-vehicle accident, requesting any station to relay to 911"

Stay on the calling frequency for emergencies - that's where people are listening!

Simplex Nets

Running a Simplex Net

Simplex nets are challenging because not all stations may hear each other. Special considerations:

Simplex Net Techniques

  • Higher power/better antenna for NCS - Net control must reach everyone
  • Central location for NCS - Geographically centered
  • Relay stations - Assign stations to relay for distant participants
  • Roll call by area - "Stations in the north sector, check in now"
  • Signal reports - Know who can hear whom
  • Backup NCS - In case primary NCS can't reach someone

Hidden Station Problem

Two stations may both hear NCS but not hear each other. Solutions:

  • NCS acknowledges all transmissions
  • Use "go ahead" permission from NCS
  • Assign relay stations
  • All stations wait for NCS before transmitting

Tips for Success

üì° Antenna

  • External antenna beats rubber duck
  • Height matters most
  • 1/2 wave or 5/8 wave for mobile
  • Mag mount on car roof helps

‚ö° Power

  • Start with low power
  • Increase if needed
  • 5W usually plenty with good antenna
  • Save battery when possible

üìç Location

  • Higher ground = better
  • Move away from buildings
  • Clear line of sight helps
  • Even a small hill helps

üîß Programming

  • Pre-program simplex freqs
  • Include in your scan list
  • Know calling freq by heart
  • Label channels clearly
Practice! Try simplex contacts regularly. Know your range to various locations BEFORE you need it in an emergency.
