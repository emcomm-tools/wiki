ICS Overview

The Incident Command System (ICS) is a standardized management system used by emergency responders across North America. Amateur radio operators supporting emergency operations should be familiar with ICS structure and forms.

Why ICS Matters for Ham Radio

  • Common Language - Everyone uses the same terminology
  • Interoperability - Work seamlessly with professional responders
  • Documentation - Proper records protect everyone
  • Accountability - Clear chain of command
  • Credibility - Professional approach builds trust
Free Training: Complete FEMA IS-100, IS-200, IS-700, and IS-800 online at training.fema.gov. Many ARES/RACES groups require these courses.

ICS-213: General Message

213

General Message Form

Purpose: Send and receive messages between any positions or locations within the ICS structure. This is the standard form for written communications.

When to Use:

  • Resource requests
  • Status reports
  • Information sharing between positions
  • Any message needing documentation
  • When radiogram format isn't required

Sample ICS-213

GENERAL MESSAGE (ICS-213)
1. Incident Name: Winter Storm Atlas
2. To: Logistics Section Chief
3. From: Shelter Manager, Lincoln School Shelter
4. Subject: Emergency Supply Request
5. Date: 2025-01-08 Time: 1430
6. Message:
Current population: 127 evacuees

Request the following supplies:
- 50 blankets (current supply exhausted)
- 200 bottles of water
- 50 MREs or equivalent meals
- 1 case of infant formula

Priority: HIGH - Expecting 50 additional evacuees by 1800.
7. Approved By: J. Smith, Shelter Manager
8. Date/Time: 2025-01-08 / 1435

ICS-213 vs Radiogram

ICS/FEMA standardAmateur radio standard

When to Use Which: Use ICS-213 for internal incident communications. Use radiogram format for NTS traffic or messages leaving the incident area through amateur radio networks.

ICS-214: Activity Log

214

Activity Log

Purpose: Document individual or unit activities during an operational period. Your personal record of what you did.

When to Use:

  • Throughout your entire deployment
  • Document arrival and departure times
  • Record significant events and actions
  • Note any safety issues or incidents
  • Track resources used or requested

ICS-214 Fields

FieldDescription
Incident NameOfficial incident name
Operational PeriodDate/time range of this log
NameYour name
ICS PositionYour assigned position
Home AgencyARES, RACES, Club name, etc.
Activity LogTime-stamped list of activities

217A

When to Use:

ICS-205: Incident Radio Communications Plan

205

Incident Radio Communications Plan

Purpose: Master plan for all radio communications during the incident. Created by the Communications Unit Leader (COML).

For Radio Operators: You'll receive this form - it tells you what frequencies to use for what purpose. Keep it handy!

Contains:

  • All assigned frequencies/channels
  • Function of each channel
  • Who uses what
  • Special instructions

ICS-205A: Communications List

ICS-309: Communications Log

309

Communications Log

Purpose: Log of all radio traffic. More detailed than ICS-214 for communications specifically.

When to Use: High-traffic operations where detailed message tracking is needed.
