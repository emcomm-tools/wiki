Battery Types

üîã LiFePO4 (Lithium Iron Phosphate)

The gold standard for portable ham radio. Safe, long-lasting, lightweight.

Nominal12.8V (4S)
Cycles2000-5000
Weight~1/3 of SLA
Self-DischargeVery Low

Pros

  • Very safe chemistry - no thermal runaway
  • Flat discharge curve - steady voltage
  • Extremely long cycle life
  • Lightweight
  • Fast charging capable

Cons

  • Higher initial cost (but best $/cycle)
  • Reduced capacity in extreme cold
  • Requires compatible charger

üîã SLA/AGM (Sealed Lead Acid)

Traditional, affordable, readily available. Heavy but reliable.

Nominal12.0V
Cycles300-500
WeightHeavy
CostLowest

Pros

  • Low cost
  • Available everywhere
  • Simple charging
  • Works in cold weather

Cons

  • Heavy!
  • Shorter cycle life
  • Voltage sags under load
  • Should only discharge to 50%

üîã Li-Ion (Lithium Ion)

Common in power stations and some packs. Good energy density.

Nominal11.1V (3S) / 14.8V (4S)
Cycles500-1000
WeightLight
EnergyHighest density

Caution

  • More sensitive to abuse than LiFePO4
  • Can be dangerous if damaged or overcharged
  • Voltage may not match 13.8V radios (check specs!)

üìä Example: POTA Activation

Scenario: 4-hour activation with IC-705 (10W SSB)

  • Receive current: 0.5A
  • Transmit current: 3A
  • Duty cycle: 30% TX, 70% RX

Calculation:

  • Average current = (0.3 √ó 3A) + (0.7 √ó 0.5A) = 0.9 + 0.35 = 1.25A
  • Required capacity = 4 hours √ó 1.25A √ó 1.2 (safety) = 6 Ah

A 9Ah LiFePO4 battery would give comfortable margin!

üìä Example: Field Day (24 hours)

Scenario: 100W HF station, heavy operation

  • Receive current: 2A
  • Transmit current: 20A
  • Duty cycle: 40% TX (contest pace)

Calculation:

  • Average current = (0.4 √ó 20A) + (0.6 √ó 2A) = 8 + 1.2 = 9.2A
  • Required capacity = 24 hours √ó 9.2A = 221 Ah!

You'll need multiple batteries, generator, or solar!

Charging Methods

Charger Types

ChargerChemistryFeatures

SLA Chargers for LiFePO4: Many AGM chargers with 14.4V float voltage work fine for LiFePO4! Avoid chargers with "desulfation" or high-voltage equalization modes.

Charge Rates

  • Slow (0.1C): Gentlest, longest battery life
  • Normal (0.2-0.5C): Typical home charging
  • Fast (1C): Quick top-up, more stress on battery
  • Example: 20Ah battery at 0.5C = 10A charge rate

Safety Considerations

Battery Safety Rules
  • Never short-circuit battery terminals
  • Use appropriate fuses (as close to battery as possible)
  • Don't mix battery types or ages
  • Don't charge damaged or swollen batteries
  • Store in fireproof container if concerned
  • Transport in approved containers (LiFePO4 generally safe)
